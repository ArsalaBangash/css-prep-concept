## TASK: Subject Page Template

All subject pages should follow this structure. Adapt content based on subject type (compulsory vs optional, 100 vs 200 marks).

### Standard Subject Page Sections

1. **Header**
   - Subject name
   - Marks (100 or 200)
   - Papers (1 or 2)
   - Pass threshold
   - Scoring trend indicator (High/Medium/Low/Variable)

2. **Overview Box**
   - 2-3 sentence description of what the subject covers
   - Why it matters for CSS
   - Who should choose it (for optionals)

3. **Syllabus Breakdown**
   - Official FPSC syllabus topics (verbatim from PDF)
   - Organized by paper (if 2 papers)
   - Weight indication per section if known

4. **Scoring Trends Section**
   - Historical scoring data (2020-2024)
   - Risk assessment (targeting warnings)
   - Volatility indicator

5. **Preparation Strategy**
   - Phase-wise approach
   - Time allocation recommendation
   - Key focus areas

6. **Recommended Resources**
   - Primary book(s) with author
   - Secondary/reference books
   - Online resources (YouTube, websites)
   - Past papers link

7. **Common Mistakes**
   - 3-5 pitfalls specific to this subject

8. **Overlap with Other Subjects**
   - Which compulsory papers it helps
   - Which optionals it synergizes with

9. **Expert Tips**
   - 2-3 actionable tips from toppers/research

10. **Related Pages**
    - Links to complementary subjects
    - Link to subject selection guide

## TASK: Psychological Assessment Guide

**Route**: `/exam/psychological` (NEW PAGE)
**Priority**: ðŸŸ¡ Medium

### Required Sections

1. **Overview**
   - Two-day assessment
   - Evaluates personality and leadership potential
   - After medical, before interview

2. **Test Components**
   - **Intelligence Tests**: Raven's Progressive Matrices
   - **WAT (Word Association Test)**: Quick word responses
   - **TAT (Thematic Apperception Test)**: Story creation from pictures
   - **Group Discussions**: Leadership observation
   - **Command Tasks**: Practical leadership exercises

3. **What They Assess**
   - Leadership potential
   - Decision-making ability
   - Emotional stability
   - Team dynamics
   - Stress response

4. **Preparation Tips**
   - Practice timed word associations
   - Develop positive story frameworks
   - Group discussion etiquette
   - Stay authentic (they detect faking)

5. **Common Misconceptions**
   - It's not pass/fail like written
   - You can't "game" it
   - Consistency matters more than "right answers"
